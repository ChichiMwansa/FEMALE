Dr. Anna Kajumulo Tibaijuka

Leave a reply

Retrieved from: https://www.sei-international.org/-news-archive/2575-gordon-goodman-lecture-with-prof-anna-tibaijuka

Dr. Anna Kajumulo Tibaijuka was born on October 12, 1950 in Muleba, Tanzania. Her parents produced coffee and bananas. Dr. Tibaijuka obtained her doctoral degree of science in Agricultural Economics from the Swedish University of Agricultural Science in Uppsala. In 2003, she received her honorary Doctorate degree in Science from the University College London (Biography of Anna TIBAIJUKA.) She is fluent in many languages: French, Swahili, Swedish, Haya, and English. Dr. Anna Tibaijuka is a member of the United Nations. As the former executive director of UN-HABITAT (or United Nations Human Settlements Programme), she was the highest ranking African woman in the organization. Since 2010, she has been a member of Parliament for Muleba South constituency. She is “Supporting the poor, jobless, young people in poverty, woman and children of Tanzania and Africa” (Mubeya). From 2010 to 2014, she served as the Minister of Lands, Housing and Human Settlement Development. In addition to her public service, Dr. Tibaijuka has published five books and several articles (http://mirror.unhabitat.org/cdrom/docs/WUF_Tibaijuka.pdf.)

While holding several leadership positions. Dr. Anna Kajumulo Tibaijuka was a member of many community organizations and held other professions. While being of the UNCTAD, United Nations Conference on Trade and Development, from 1993 to 1998, she was the associate professor of economics at the University of Dar-es-Salaam. Dr. Tibaijuka served as a Tanzanian Government delegate to the United Nations. She was a member of the NGO Forums, UNESCO’s International Scientific Advisory Board, and the Civil Society. She had been on UNESCO’s International Scientific Advisory Board since 1997. Dr. Tibaijuka was elected as the Coordinator for Eastern Africa in the Network for Food Security, Trade, and Sustainable Development. She founded two organizations: the Tanzanian National Women’s Council, or BAWATA, and the Barbro Johansson Girls Education Trust. Dr. Tibaijuka is the founded of Tanzania’s Local Entrepreneurs Initiative, or TALE.

As the former highest ranking woman in the United Nations, she has overseen many changes in the organization. In 2000, she became the Executive Director of UN-HABITAT. By 2002, she led the reform to change the program from the United Nations Center for Human Settlements to the UN program, UN-HABITAT. Under her leadership, UN-HABITAT has greatly improved, becoming more visible to the public and the program’s member states. She has been a huge part of the program’s effort to add the homeless and less fortunate across the world. (http://mirror.unhabitat.org/cdrom/docs/WUF_Tibaijuka.pdf)

Dr. Tibaijuko is a huge advocate of women’s rights. BAWATA and Barbri Johansson Girls Education Trust focuses on promoting and encouraging women to pursue education. Dr. Anna Kajumulo Tibaijuka is a great example of a Black Leader. She is an African Women who has a significant amount of power in one of the biggest world organizations, the United Nations. Dr. Tibaijuko has made a huge impact in the lives of women, Africans, and those living poverty. Many times, when we think about Black leaders, we think about leaders from the past, but aside from President Barack Obama we don’t talk about other Black who are currently making a difference in their communities and the world. Dr. Anna Kajumulo Tibaijuka is a leader and should be recognized for her efforts, passion, and dedication.
